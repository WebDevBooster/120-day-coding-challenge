Sooo?.... What do you think?

Do you think you can manage to leave this page
without taking the action I'm forcing your brain to take?

Maybe you can because I'm posing it as a challenge now.
But what if you landed on a web page with some other content
and nobody would even mention anything about that info icon?

Also important: Would you feel in any way annoyed if that info icon just
rolled out "out of the blue" and started "calling" for your action the way my info icon does?

Doesn't feel annyoing, right? :-)

What would normally happen is that the user would first move their mouse over the info icon
(without necessarily having the intention to click it).
And once there, the user would think:
"What the heck! I might just as well click it now!"

And that, my friend, is the art of persuasion and the art of website conversion.

Subtle things like this innocent looking little info icon that can make a difference between a
business website that's barely making ends meet and a
business website that outsells all of their competition!

I'm not saying you should put this info icon onto every business website. No.
What I am saying is that:
1) I have a very clear understanding of who exactly the visitors of my project pages are and
2) I have a very clear idea of what exactly I want those visitors to do
when they land on any of my project pages.

I then come up with the things I need to implement in a given case in order
to virtually force the users to do exactly what I want them to do
and do that without being obnoxious.

The job of a typical business website is to make money for the business.
And you do that by converting website visitors into leads and sales.

Now, "leads" in my case here means something totally different
than you'd typically have on a business website.
And actually, I have 2 very different kinds of leads in my case here.

But even though those are 2 very different groups of people
I want both of those user groups to take the action I prescribed for them to take
which is to click the info icon and to consume the easy-to-digest information
that will pop up upon clicking that icon.

After that the users of those 2 user groups will diverge and perform different actions
(depending on the group they belong to).
But getting all those people to click the info icon is the first and critically important step
when it comes to converting those users into leads.
